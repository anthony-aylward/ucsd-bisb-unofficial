This page is for discussion of doctoral committies.

One thing many BISB students don't learn until a couple of years into their Ph.D is that you'll need two advisors. This requirement is written on the official website, however tersely:

Each graduate student in the program will work on a dissertation project under dual mentorship, consisting of a primary advisor who is Program Training Faculty, and a co-advisor who may or may not be Program Training Faculty, but must be from a different disciplinary area.

The typical interpretation of the "different disciplinary area" clause is that if the advisor is computationally focused then the co-advisor should be experimentally focused and vice versa. However, there is no formal definition of what this means.

When choosing your advisor and co-advisor, consider talking to older students about pairs they've chosen and what has or hasn't been successful.
